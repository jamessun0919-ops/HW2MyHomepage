Customer Data Integration

Restaurant brands today mainly draw customer information from two sources: reservation data and membership/checkout data. Even though the market offers many mainstream restaurant information systems, and even POS vendors that claim to be "all-in-one" typically still manage membership through a self-built reservation system connected to LINE, so real customer data ends up scattered across different places.

The main advantage of this feature is that it doesn't require installing any new system — it simply merges the data files produced by different systems, removes useless system-generated code columns, and keeps/generates the actually useful fields based on the user's needs, making the data far easier to read and analyze.

Solves the pain point of information silos between departments:

As mentioned above, a company's division of labor can mean "membership spending data" lives in a membership system like Ocard or LINE, managed by the marketing department, while "reservation data" lives in a POS system like Dodu/iChef, or a standalone system like Inline, managed by the operations department. This information gap creates information silos, preventing the data from being fully utilized.

Precisely targeted marketing messages:

Analysis of actual customer spending behavior shows that for group consumption such as family gatherings or coworker meals, the person who made the reservation and the person who pays the bill are usually not the same person, which is what causes the customer-data discrepancy mentioned above. A single consumption event ends up producing two separate customer records, and under a traditional marketing workflow, that means paying for two marketing messages instead of one.

The situation above can't be handled manually, but AI can analyze clues such as consumption time and behavior to merge and link the records. This produces a more precise customer profile (the one who organizes the gathering vs. the one who pays), enabling marketing messages to be targeted more precisely.
